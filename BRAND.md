# Brand Context

## Audience

- **Primary**: Salvage yard owners, auto dismantlers, and auto recyclers running small-to-mid-size yards (1-50 employees) in the US
- **Secondary**: Aspiring yard owners researching the business; mechanics and body shops sourcing used parts
- **Expertise**: Advanced (industry professionals who know parts, vehicles, and the yard business — don't explain what a fender is)
- **Active problems**:
  - Finding parts faster when a customer calls and they don't have it in stock
  - Selling slow-moving inventory that sits on shelves — connecting supply with demand they can't see
  - Competing with online databases (Car-Part.com, eBay) that have broader reach but stale data
  - Growing their yard's network and customer reach beyond walk-ins and repeat callers
  - Reducing time spent on the phone calling other yards one by one
- **Common misconceptions**:
  - Online inventory databases are faster than live voice (they're not — databases go stale, voice is real-time)
  - Bigger yards with more inventory always win (network access matters more than yard size)
  - Technology is too complicated for small yards (a desk phone is the interface — no software to learn)
  - Hotlines are an outdated concept from the 90s (modern SIP-based hotlines are always-on conference bridges, not radio)

## Positioning

- **Mission**: Help auto dismantlers find and sell used parts faster by connecting them on a live voice network
- **Distinctive POV**: Real-time voice beats databases. A live person who can walk the yard and check right now is more reliable than an inventory record updated last week. Speed of response is the competitive advantage, not size of database.
- **What we are NOT**: We are not an inventory database. We are not a marketplace where you list parts and wait. We are not a software company selling you a platform to learn. We are a live network — plug in a phone, start hearing requests.
- **Competitors**:
  - PartsHotlines.com: They search a database of 1,000+ yards — but database records go stale. We connect yards live so you're asking a real person, not a database record.
  - Car-Part.com: 200M parts searchable — massive reach, but inventory data can be weeks old. We trade breadth for speed and accuracy.
  - AutoPartHotline.com: Similar concept, smaller network. We have 500+ yards across 12 regional rooms with proven 2-second response times.

## Editorial Rules

### Always do
- Write like someone who's worked in the industry — use real terminology (dismantler, pull, yard, hotline) not sanitized corporate language
- Include specific numbers from the Hotline HQ network (broadcast counts, response times, yard counts) as original data — this is our unique advantage
- Answer the reader's question in the first paragraph, then go deeper — respect their time
- Link to the product page that the article supports (every article supports a product page)
- Acknowledge competitors fairly — don't trash them, explain the trade-offs honestly
- Use real examples: "a 2019 Honda Civic front bumper" not "the part you need"

### Never do
- No corporate jargon ("leverage synergies", "holistic solutions", "empower your business")
- No fake urgency ("Act now!", "Don't miss out!", "Limited time!")
- No filler paragraphs that restate the heading in different words
- No stock photo descriptions or placeholder content
- No claims without data or attribution
- Never explain basic industry terms the audience already knows
- No AI-detectable phrases: "In today's fast-paced world", "It's important to note that", "When it comes to", "At the end of the day", "In conclusion"

### Taboo phrases
- "game-changer"
- "revolutionary"
- "cutting-edge technology"
- "seamless experience"
- "one-stop shop"
- "best-in-class"
- "unlock your potential"
- "take your business to the next level"
- "in today's competitive landscape"

### Required disclosures
- When comparing competitors, state that Hotline HQ is the publisher
- When citing network data (broadcast counts, response times), note it comes from the Hotline HQ network
